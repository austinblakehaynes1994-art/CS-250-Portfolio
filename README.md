## Reflection

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by first understanding what the user is trying to accomplish. Instead of only thinking about features, I think about the problem the user needs solved. User stories help with this because they explain the feature from the user’s point of view. A user story makes it clear who the user is, what they need, and why they need it. This helps developers build software that has a real purpose and meets the user’s expectations.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by breaking the work into smaller parts and focusing on one goal at a time. This makes the project easier to plan, test, and improve. In the future, I hope to use Agile processes such as sprint planning, daily communication, sprint reviews, and retrospectives. These processes help teams stay organized, respond to change, and improve the product throughout development instead of waiting until the end.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being dependable, and respecting the work of others. A good team member completes their responsibilities, asks questions when needed, and helps solve problems instead of creating more confusion. It also means being open to feedback and willing to improve. Software development works best when the team stays honest, organized, and focused on the same goal.
